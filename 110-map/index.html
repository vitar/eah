    ‚≠ê Translation Map: Connecting Estimation Methods Across the Delivery Lifecycle¶

    A unified framework that aligns estimation for RFP, discovery, delivery, forecasting, and value.

    1. Purpose of This Translation Map¶

    Every stage of the lifecycle uses a different estimation method:

    • Pre-sales ‚Üí FTE & cost
    • Delivery ‚Üí story points & velocity
    • Forecasting ‚Üí flow metrics
    • Trade-offs ‚Üí value vs. effort
    • Design/Architecture ‚Üí discovery workload

    Historically, these methods do not align, creating:

    • mismatched expectations
    • drift between RFP and delivery
    • inflated velocity
    • late scope cuts
    • unpredictable timelines

    This map creates a single coherent translation layer grounded in Complexity and Uncertainty, so each method reinforces the others.

    2. The Translation Challenge (Why We Need This Map)¶

    Common questions teams face:

    • ‚ÄúHow does the cost estimate connect to story points?‚Äù
    • ‚ÄúWhy does velocity go up but timeline still slips?‚Äù
    • ‚ÄúWhy does RFP complexity disappear during delivery?‚Äù
    • ‚ÄúWhy is design always under-estimated?‚Äù
    • ‚ÄúHow do we detect when scope has actually changed?‚Äù

    This map answers them.

    3. The Core Model: Two Dimensions, Three Outputs¶

    Input Dimensions¶

    1. Complexity (known work)
    2. Uncertainty (unknown work)

    Three Outputs¶

    1. Initial effort (RFP)
    2. Delivery effort (story points & flow)
    3. Forecasting (probabilistic timelines)

    These outputs must be derived from the same backbone.

    4. Mapping Complexity to Different Estimation Methods¶

    Below are the most common methods in your organization and how complexity connects them.

    4.1. Complexity ‚Üí FTE Estimates (RFP stage)¶

    At RFP, we estimate cost in FTE-weeks/months.

    Translation:¶

    Complexity Score determines the multiplier applied to base FTE effort.

    Example multiplier table:

    Complexity Sum Multiplier
    0‚Äì3 √ó1.0
    4‚Äì7 √ó1.2
    8‚Äì12 √ó1.5
    13+ √ó2.0

    Example:¶

    • Base effort = 2 dev-weeks
    • Complexity = 10 ‚Üí multiplier √ó1.5
    • Total = 3 dev-weeks (before uncertainty)

    This is clean, defensible, and scalable.

    4.2. Complexity ‚Üí Story Points (Delivery stage)¶

    Story points don‚Äôt disappear. They become a refinement of the complexity baseline.

    Translation:¶

    Complexity levels anchor SP ranges.

    Complexity Level SP Range
    Low (1‚Äì3) 1‚Äì3
    Medium (4‚Äì7) 5‚Äì8
    High (8‚Äì12) 13‚Äì21
    Extreme (13+) >21 or split

    This prevents silent scope inflation hidden inside SP re-estimation.

    4.3. Complexity ‚Üí Team Composition & Skills¶

    A high complexity driver such as:

    • integration
    • domain logic
    • NFR
    • compliance
    • unclear ownership

    ‚Ä¶means we need correct staffing early.

    Translation examples:¶

    • High integration complexity ‚Üí Architect involvement full cycle
    • High UX complexity ‚Üí PD workload increases
    • High domain logic complexity ‚Üí BA workload increases

    This corrects the blind spot where PM/PD/BA effort is assumed rather than estimated.

    4.4. Complexity ‚Üí Design & Discovery Effort¶

    Discovery is not ‚Äúoverhead.‚Äù It is complexity translation.

    Translation:¶

    • High domain complexity ‚Üí longer BA clarification
    • High UX complexity ‚Üí more PD design cycles
    • High integration complexity ‚Üí architecture mapping
    • High uncertainty ‚Üí dependency mapping, spikes

    This links discovery/design to reality instead of guessing.

    5. Mapping Uncertainty Across Methods¶

    Uncertainty is a distinct dimension. It affects:

    • risk
    • confidence
    • cost buffers
    • timeline ranges
    • probability of scope drift

    5.1. Uncertainty ‚Üí Risk Modifiers (RFP)¶

    Example multipliers:

    Uncertainty Score Multiplier
    0‚Äì1 √ó1.0
    2 √ó1.3
    3 √ó1.6
    5 √ó2.0

    Example:¶

    Effort after complexity = 3 dev-weeks Uncertainty multiplier = √ó1.6 Final = 4.8 ‚Üí round to 5 dev-weeks

    5.2. Uncertainty ‚Üí PERT Ranges (best / likely / worst)¶

    Uncertainty maps directly to variance.

    • Low uncertainty ‚Üí tight range
    • High uncertainty ‚Üí wide range

    Example:

    • Likely: 5 weeks
    • Low U ‚Üí 4‚Äì6
    • Medium U ‚Üí 3‚Äì8
    • High U ‚Üí 2‚Äì12

    This avoids the lie of a single-scenario estimate.

    5.3. Uncertainty ‚Üí Discovery Workload (delivery readiness)¶

    High uncertainty automatically means:

    • spikes
    • PoCs
    • stakeholder sessions
    • alignment workshops
    • design explorations

    Teams can no longer skip clarification work.

    5.4. Uncertainty ‚Üí Probability of Drift¶

    This becomes the early-warning indicator.

    If uncertainty stays high during delivery ‚Üí timeline will slip.

    7. Mapping to Forecasting¶

    Complexity ‚Üí batch size¶

    Higher complexity = larger stories = larger cycle time

    Uncertainty ‚Üí variance¶

    Higher uncertainty = wider spread in Monte Carlo simulation

    Complexity baseline ‚Üí throughput¶

    Provides a clear comparison of planned vs. actual

    Complexity drift ‚Üí early-warning signals¶

    If drift grows faster than throughput, the project is behind.

    8. Mapping to Value & Trade-Offs¶

    This model makes prioritization empirical:

    High-value, low-complexity ‚Üí do immediately¶

    High-value, high-complexity ‚Üí invest carefully / scope slices¶

    Low-value, high-complexity ‚Üí cut early¶

    Low-value, low-complexity ‚Üí opportunistic¶

    This matches the 3SF value cycle (product ‚Üî client).

    9. Mapping to 3SF (3-in-3 SDLC Framework)¶

    Engagement Line (client ‚Üî vendor)¶

    • Complexity & Uncertainty create transparent RFP conversations
    • Fewer surprises
    • More realistic proposals
    • Trust increases

    Delivery Line (vendor ‚Üî product)¶

    • Story points become meaningful
    • Velocity pressure decreases
    • Complexity drift becomes visible
    • Predictability increases

    Value Line (product ‚Üî client)¶

    • Tradeoffs become transparent
    • Prioritization becomes data-driven
    • Scope decisions align to business outcomes
    • Value delivery becomes measurable

    This model strengthens all 3 connections in the 3SF triangle.
